UNIVERSAL EXPERIENCE FLOW STRUCTURE

ALL crafts must now follow this structure:

FLOW STAGE 1 — ENTRY SEQUENCE

When users first click a craft:

DO NOT instantly show menus or products.

Instead:
launch a cinematic atmospheric introduction.

The environment should:
• fade in slowly
• establish emotional tone
• animate atmospheric visuals
• reveal craft identity
• introduce the experience philosophy

Examples:

POURCRAFT 360

Warm gold reflections.
Liquid movement.
Soft jazz/lounge atmosphere.
Crystal reflections.
Slow cinematic camera motion.

Voice/text intro:
“Tonight is not about ordering a drink.
It’s about crafting an experience.”

⸻

BrewCraft 360

Copper/brass atmosphere.
Barrel textures.
Foam movement.
Industrial luxury brewery energy.

Voice/text intro:
“Every brew tells a story.
Tonight, you discover yours.”

⸻

VapeCraft 360

Neon haze.
Reactive vapor trails.
Futuristic ambient visuals.

Voice/text intro:
“Flavor is atmosphere.
Atmosphere is experience.”

⸻

FLOW STAGE 2 — EXPERIENCE EXPLANATION

Each craft must explain:

• what this experience is
• how progression works
• what users unlock
• how discovery works
• how mastery works
• how pairings work
• how personalization evolves

This should feel:
luxurious and mysterious,
NOT instructional manuals.

Examples:
• unlock hidden journeys
• evolve your taste identity
• discover reserve experiences
• build your sensory profile
• master flavor families
• create your signature path

⸻

FLOW STAGE 3 — LEVEL SELECTION

Users choose their experience level.

This controls:
• UI complexity
• educational depth
• recommendation complexity
• progression speed
• sensory guidance

Example structure:

POURCRAFT 360

• Curious Guest
• Cocktail Explorer
• Spirit Enthusiast
• Mixology Scholar
• Reserve Master

⸻

BrewCraft 360

• Casual Drinker
• Craft Curious
• Hop Explorer
• Beer Scholar
• Master Taster

⸻

VapeCraft 360

• Flavor Curious
• Cloud Explorer
• Atmosphere Builder
• Flavor Architect
• Sensory Master

⸻

FLOW STAGE 4 — TASTE / MOOD DISCOVERY

Users enter guided discovery.

The system asks:
• mood
• flavor direction
• atmosphere preference
• social energy
• strength preference
• experience goal

This must feel conversational and immersive.

NOT forms.

The UI should:
• glide
• react
• animate softly
• feel emotionally responsive

⸻

FLOW STAGE 5 — EXPERIENCE PATH GENERATION

The system creates:
a personalized journey path.

Examples:

POURCRAFT 360

“Tonight’s Journey”
• Discovery Pour
• Flavor Expansion
• Signature Cocktail
• Reserve Unlock

⸻

BrewCraft 360

“Tonight’s Flight”
• Regional Discovery
• Barrel Exploration
• Rare Brew Unlock
• Pairing Finish

⸻

VapeCraft 360

“Tonight’s Atmosphere”
• Flavor Base
• Cloud Layer
• Ambient Sync
• Sensory Enhancement

⸻

FLOW STAGE 6 — LIVE INTERACTION EXPERIENCE

NOW users enter:
the active craft experience.

This is where:
• recommendations appear
• crafting happens
• unlocks occur
• bartender/brewer interaction happens
• social interactions activate
• environmental reactions trigger

This must feel:
alive and evolving.

⸻

FLOW STAGE 7 — EDUCATIONAL DISCOVERY

Education must happen THROUGH interaction.

Examples:
• unlock spirit history during crafting
• reveal brewing science during tasting
• explain terpene chemistry during flavor building

Never:
large information walls.

The user should feel:
they discovered the knowledge themselves.

⸻

FLOW STAGE 8 — PROGRESSION + REWARDS

After interaction:
users gain:
• progression
• unlocks
• new pathways
• flavor mastery
• hidden experiences
• signature status

The reward system should feel:
exclusive and luxurious.

NOT arcade-style.

⸻

FLOW STAGE 9 — EXPERIENCE RECAP

At the end:
generate a cinematic recap.

Examples:

POURCRAFT 360

“Tonight you explored:
Smoked Vanilla,
Dark Cherry,
Aged Tequila,
and Reserve Oak profiles.”

⸻

BrewCraft 360

“You unlocked:
Belgian Barrel Discovery
and Hop Profile Expansion.”

⸻

VapeCraft 360

“You evolved your:
Citrus Vapor Signature.”

The recap should:
encourage return visits and future discovery.

⸻

FLOW STAGE 10 — RETURN USER FLOW

Returning users should experience:

“Welcome back.”

The system remembers:
• preferences
• progression
• favorite paths
• prior unlocks
• sensory profile

Users can:
• continue journeys
• revisit favorites
• unlock advanced paths
• challenge new experiences

⸻

SOCIAL EXPERIENCE FLOW

• multiplayer flavor voting

The system should support:
interactive nightlife experiences.

⸻

ENVIRONMENTAL IMMERSION SYSTEM

Each flow stage should affect:
• lighting visuals
• motion pacing
• particle effects
• sound atmosphere
• environmental overlays
• emotional ambiance

The interface should feel:
responsive to the journey itself.

⸻

FINAL SYSTEM GOAL

The user should feel:
• immersed
• guided
• elevated
• educated
• entertained
• emotionally connected

The venue should feel:
alive,
intelligent,
and unforgettable.

The experience should feel less like:
using software

and more like:
entering a premium interactive world powered by Axi

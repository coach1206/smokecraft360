The "Sensory Override" Prompt: SmokeCraft Visuals & Voice
Objective: Rewrite the card rendering and background logic to eliminate "dull" cards and replace them with high-fidelity, sensory-accurate content that matches the flavor profile.

1. Flavor-Responsive Card Imagery
The Problem: Cards currently use generic images or gradients when product images are null.

The Fix: Implement a Flavor Mapping Engine. The card image must reflect the tags (Smoky, Bold, Creamy, Nutty).

Smoky & Bold: Use high-contrast, macro shots of thick, swirling white smoke against dark wood.

Creamy: Use soft-focus, lighter-toned imagery of silky smoke or a buttery-colored wrapper.

Nutty: Use images featuring warm, earthy brown textures and roasted coffee/almond-colored smoke cues.

Logic: If item.image is null, the resolveCardImage must look at item.flavorProfile and pull from a specific "Flavor Pool" of cinematic shots, ensuring the visual matches the text 1:1.

2. Background Visibility & "The Living Atmosphere"
The Problem: The background image is too dark or washed out.

The Fix: Implement a Dynamic Backdrop.

The background should be 20% more visible than the current version but with a "Glassmorphism" blur to ensure text remains readable.

The Motion: Backgrounds must be in subtle motion—slow-drifting smoke or amber liquid swirling.

Sync: When a user swiped to a "Bold" card, the background should subtly shift to a deeper, more intense amber glow.

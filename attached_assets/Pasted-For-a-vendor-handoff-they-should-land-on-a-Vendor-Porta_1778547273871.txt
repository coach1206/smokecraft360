For a vendor handoff, they should land on a Vendor Portal, not the Sovereign Command Center.

The vendor should see something like:

Vendor Handoff Page

Route:
/vendor/onboarding for first-time setup
then
/vendor/dashboard after approval/login

What the vendor sees first
1. Welcome / Vendor Setup

Page title:

Welcome to the EEIE Vendor Portal

Simple message:

“You’ve been invited to connect your products, inventory, and media to the EEIE experience system. This portal lets you manage only your approved products, images, availability, and performance.”

Buttons:

Start Vendor Setup
Upload Product Catalog
Add Product Images
View Approval Status

After they complete setup, they see the Vendor Dashboard
Vendor Dashboard Sections

1. Vendor Overview
Their company name, approval status, active products, assigned venues, pending items.

2. Product Catalog
Their cigars, liquor, food, desserts, accessories, or other assigned product types.

They can:

Add product
Edit product
Upload image
Set availability
Mark low stock
Submit for approval

3. Media Uploads
This is where they upload clean product images.

They can upload:

Cigar images
Cigar box images
Liquor bottle images
Cocktail images
Food images
Dessert images
Bundle images
Brand logos

But those images should go into pending approval before going live.

4. Inventory Status
They see only their inventory.

Example:

Cohiba Behike 56
12 available
3 assigned to venue
Low stock alert: no

Woodford Reserve Double Oaked
8 bottles available
2 featured in pairings
Status: active

5. Assigned Venues
They see where their products are approved to appear.

Example:

SmokeCraft Lounge Atlanta
Approved products: 12
Pending media: 3
Active pairings: 4

They should not see other venues unless assigned.

6. Pairing Performance
They can see how their products are performing, but only their products.

Example:

Cohiba Behike 56
Shown to guests: 42
Added to cart: 18
Conversion: 43%
Revenue influenced: $850

7. Approval Status
They see what is waiting on admin approval.

Pending:

3 product images
2 new products
1 price update
1 bundle request

8. Messages / Requests
Managers or admin can request:

new product images
updated pricing
restock confirmation
new catalog upload
promotional placement

What the vendor should NOT see

They should never see:

Sovereign Command Center
Founder Control
Master Operations
Revoke All
Distribution Vault controls
Hardware Lab
Keys
Live Nodes
Deploy
War Room
global revenue
other vendors
other venues
system security
staff cockpit
guest profiles
POS controls

Clean handoff flow

The flow should be:

Admin invites vendor
↓
Vendor gets link
↓
Vendor lands on /vendor/onboarding
↓
Vendor fills company profile
↓
Vendor uploads catalog and images
↓
Admin approves products/media
↓
Products become available inside EEIE Product Wall, Staff Cockpit, and Pairing Engine
↓
Vendor tracks only their own product performance inside /vendor/dashboard

Prompt for Replit

Create a vendor-safe handoff flow for EEIE / Sovereign.

Vendors must never see the Sovereign Command Center, Founder Control, Master Operations, Distribution Vault, Hardware Lab, War Room, Keys, Live Nodes, Deploy, or any global admin controls.

Create these vendor routes:

/vendor/dashboard
/vendor/products
/vendor/media

/vendor/performance

Vendor onboarding page must show:

Welcome to the EEIE Vendor Portal
company setup

product category selection
catalog upload

submit for approval

Vendor dashboard must show only vendor-safe data:

Vendor Overview
Product Catalog
Inventory Status
Media Uploads
Assigned Venues
Approval Status
Messages
Product Performance

Vendor users can only view and manage their own products, images, inventory, approvals, and performance.

Create role guard:

vendor users may access only /vendor/* routes.

If vendor attempts to access /sovereign-dashboard, /founder-control, /master-operations, /distribution-vault, /hardware-lab, /war-room, /keys, /live-nodes, or /deploy, redirect them to /vendor/dashboard.

Show message:

Vendor access is limited to your approved vendor portal.

Vendor product images must go into pending approval before appearing in Staff Cockpit, Product Wall, Media Library, or Pairing Engine.

Vendor performance must only show their own products.

No global revenue.
No guest profiles.
No staff controls.
No hardware controls.
No founder controls.
No deployment controls.
No cross-vendor data.

So the vendor handoff page should be a clean Vendor Portal, focused on product catalog, images, inventory, approvals, and performance. It should feel premium, but locked down.

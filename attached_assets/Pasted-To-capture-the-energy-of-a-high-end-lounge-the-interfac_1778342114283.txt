To capture the energy of a high-end lounge, the interface needs to shift from a "form" to a "cinematic ritual." If the room is loud and the smoke is thick, the software should feel like a high-stakes game—tactile, responsive, and visually addictive.

Here is a redesigned flow for the Axiom OS: SmokeCraft module, focusing on high-stimulation visuals and a real-time "Master Blender" feedback loop.

1. The Living Dashboard (The "Hook")
Instead of a static menu, the screen features a Dynamic Progress Halo. As the user makes choices, a glowing ring (Gold/Amber) fills up toward 100% Synergy.

The Pulse: Every tap triggers a haptic vibration and a visual ripple across the dark glass UI.

Real-time Feedback: A "Flavor Profile" radar graph sits in the corner, shifting its shape (Spicy, Sweet, Earthy, Bold) instantly as choices are made.

2. The Selection Ritual (High-Stimulation Visuals)
We replace text lists with a high-definition, interactive gallery.

Stage	Visual Element	Interaction
The Wrapper	Ultra-HD macros of Connecticut Shade, Habano, and Maduro leaves.	Users swipe through physical textures. You can see the veins and the oil on the leaf.
The Vitola	3D renders of Parejos (Robusto, Toro, Churchill) and Figurados (Belicoso, Torpedo).	A "Smoke Time" slider (30 min – 2 hours) physically grows or shrinks the cigar image.
The Preparation	High-contrast images of a Straight Cut, V-Cut, and Punch.	Selecting a cut triggers a "snip" sound effect and a close-up animation of the cap being removed.
3. The "Blender’s Insight" Engine
Once the tobacco profile is set, the system "brews" the recommendation.

The Reveal: The screen goes dark, then "scans" the venue's live humidor inventory.

The Match: It presents the top three cigars in stock that hit the 100/100 mark.

The Alchemy: Below the cigar, a "Perfect Pour" animation appears. If the cigar is a spicy Ligero, the screen might suggest a high-proof Bourbon or a smoky Islay Scotch, showing a slow-motion pour of the amber liquid into a crystal glencairn.

4. Gamification & Points
To keep them staring at the screen, the points need to feel like "Leveling Up."

Point Pop-ups: "+15 XP: Boldness Architect" or "+10 XP: Wrapper Specialist" appear in gold text as they navigate.

The "Final Boss" Screen: Upon completion, the 100/100 score triggers a "Master Blender" badge.

The Interaction: A "Send to Server" button pulses with a glassmorphism glow, making the act of ordering feel like launching a mission.

UI Motion Guidelines
Everything in Motion: Backgrounds should feature subtle, looping "cinemagraphs" of slow-rising cigar smoke and swirling spirits.

Feedback Loops: When a user hits 100/100, the interface should briefly "flare" with gold light to signal success.

No Scrolling: Everything should be swipeable or "tap-to-expand" to keep the user engaged with the center of the tablet screen.
